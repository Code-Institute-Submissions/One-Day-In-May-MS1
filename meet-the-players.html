  • Position: CM
  • Signed: Sheffield Wednesday
  • Appearances: 273

  • Magilton initially joined Ipswich on-loan from Sheffield Wednesday for the second half of the 1998-99 season. He impressed in his first 11 Appearances and the move was made permanent in March 1999. His only career hattrick came in the second play-off semi-final against Bolton. Magilton spent 7 years with Ipswich, captaining the club in his final 3 seasons following the sale of Matt Holland. Magilton retired at the end of the 2005-06 season and was appointed as Ipswich manager in June 2006.

Marcus Stewart
  • Shirt Number: 27
  • DOB: 08/11/1972
  • Position: ST
  • Signed: Huddersfield Town
  • Appearances: 75
  • Goals: 27
  • Stewart arrived from Huddersfield Town in February 2000 to strengthen Ipswich's front line for the promotion run-in. Huddersfield were promotion rivals of Ipswich, having topped the table at Christmas. Stewart had netted 15 times for the Terriers so his sale did not go down well with their supporters. For Ipswich, Stewart proved the investment was worth it with two crucial goals in the first play-off semi-final and another in the play-off final at Wembley. He would notch 19 Premier League goals the following campaign and was the highest scoring Englishman in the league as Ipswich finished 5th.

  • Ipswich fans love an exciting Dutch midfielder! Reuser joined initially on-loan from Ajax before signing permanently following Ipswich's prommotion in the summer of 2000. Reuser only scored 19 goals in just over 4 seasons with Ipswich but many of them were memorable strikes. He netted a winner on his debut followed by two strikes in the play-offs, each of which were goals that cemented victory. He gained a reputation as a free-kick specialist after curling in a couple of excellent strikes during Ipswich's first season back in the Premier League. The midfielder was always exciting to watch but injury problems hampered him during his time with the Blues.

Richard Naylor
  • Shirt Number: 12
  • DOB: 28/02/1977
  • Position: ST
  • Signed: -
  • Appearances: 373
  • Goals: 41
  • Of the 1999-2000 promotion winning squad, Naylor would be the longest servant of the club, remaining with Ipswich until 2009 having emerged from the academy in 1996. Naylor started his career as a centre-forward where his physical approach earned him the nickname "Bamm-Bamm" after the agressive Flintstones toddler. As a striker, Naylor largely featured as back-up for Town's first choice forwards. In 2002, he had brief loan spells at Millwall and ironically, Barnsley. Following Ipswich's relegation in 2002, Naylor came back into the team when Burley's successor Joe Royle converted him into a robust centre-back, a position he would play for the remainder of his career. In September 2006, Naylor was given a testimonial which saw the current squad draw 3-3 with a Wembley 2000 XI.
